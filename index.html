Introduction To Physics

- What is Physics?

Physics is the study of phenomenon that occur in our day to day lives. It is the branch of science that deals with the study of matter, energy, motion, gravitation etc.


- What is the meaning of physcis?

The term "physics" originates from the Greek word "physis," which means "nature" or "natural." In its broadest sense, physics is the scientific study of the fundamental principles governing the behavior of matter and energy in the universe. It encompasses the investigation of various phenomena, including motion, forces, energy, heat, light, sound, electricity, magnetism, and the structure of atoms and the universe as a whole. Physics seeks to understand the fundamental laws and principles that govern the behavior of objects, from the smallest subatomic particles to the largest celestial bodies. It aims to describe and predict the behavior of natural phenomena through the formulation of mathematical models, theories, and experimental observations. Overall, physics is a fundamental science that forms the basis for many other scientific disciplines and technological advancements. It plays a crucial role in shaping our understanding of the natural world and has profound implications for technology, engineering, medicine, and many other fields.

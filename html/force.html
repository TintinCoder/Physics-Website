FORCE

- What is Force?

Force is defined as a push or pull that tends to change the position, size, shape, and state of motion. Forces have mainly two types based on the total force acting on the body.


- The SI unit of Force is 'N'(Newton). It is named after the scientist Sir Isaac Newton.


- Net Force or Resultant Force

Net force or the resultant force is the total amount of force acting on a body. For Example - If A pushes a body with the force of 10N and B pushes the same object with the force of 20N from the opposite direction, the net force would be of (20N+(-10N)) = 10N in the direction of A.


- Types of Forces based on the net force acting on the body

Balanced Force
- When the net force acting on the body becomes 0.
Unbalanced Force
- When the net force acting on the body is not 0.

- Difference between balanced force and Unbalanced force

Balanced Force Unbalanced Force
When the net force acting on a body is 0. When the net force acting on a body is not 0.
It is responsible for no change in the shape, size, and state of motion of a body. It is reponsible for the change in the shape, size, or state of motion of a body.

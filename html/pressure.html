Pressure

- What is pressure?

Pressure is defined as the force acting on an object per unit area.


- Pressure is inversly proportional to the area of the object

Pressure increases when the area decreases and pressure decreases when the area increases. For example - A sharp knife can cut something easily as compared to a blunt knife as the area of sharp knife is very less and on the other hand the area of blunt knife is large.


- The SI unit of pressure is Pa(Pascal)


- Formula for Pressure

The formula of pressure is force(N) per unit area(m²).
P = F A
